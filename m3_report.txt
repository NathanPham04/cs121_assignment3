not in the posting lists. In old_search.py, the reason that the bad queries are so much faster is because if the tokens in the query are not in the posting lists, then they are 
just skipped, which deletes a look up operation from the algorithm. 

In search.py, we switched to using partial indexes rather than the full one. This dramatically increased the lookup time for both good and bad queries. The "bad" queries are still 
faster, but not there is less of a gap now between the mean of the good and bad because it doesn't have to search through the entire index at once.
